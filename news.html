The mut player player count has been on a relatively fixed cycle over the years, revolving around a spike in popularity from The International Battle Pass. 2019 saw the mut player count plunge after the end of the battle pass, which led to historically low player counts in January 2020.

Spring 2020 saw business pick up in mut and a number of other esports titles, but the game entered into a steady decline in May despite the launch of the TI10 Battle Pass. That put mut on pace to dip down to numbers from early on in the game’s existence, but Valve has managed to head off that decline.

The TI10 Battle Pass’ guild feature was made a permanent part of the game and was tied into an expansion of the mut Plus shard system. This gave players rewards for continued play in a way that hadn’t really existed prior. On October 29, Valve gave fans another treat by bringing back the Diretide game mode for the first time since 2013. This combination could be the start of a climb back to relevance for mut
